Film Bang 1976-2020 - Resilience and Sustainability for Freelance Careers in the Screen Industries


Alistair Scott & Neil Davidson

What have we done?

  • Quantitative

    Charting Gender Breakdown of workforce, departmental growth, longevity, turnover,the makeup of each department

  • Qualitative

    Querying the current Film Bang community with an online survey, and interviewing a range Film Bang freelancers

Just under a 3rd of all entries are for 1 year. Half of entries are 3 years or less. 75% of entries are in for less than 8 years

Challenges

  • Reliance on published historic material with no form of verification

  • Gender inferred according to name or anecdotal evidence

  • Presence in the directory is not a proof of work

  • No data on diversity in terms of ethnicity, disability, age, background, gender+, sexuality

  • Geographic location is only recorded up to 2015

  • Listings 1976-1989 were mandatory due to union rules. Since 1990 listings are voluntary

Policy Proposals:

  • Co-ordinated engagement with other stakeholders

  • HMRC to review employment status of freelancers in film and television

  • Recognition of the interdependency of freelancers and the broadcasters and larger independent companies

  • Co-ordinated & integrated industry approach to training, support, retraining

  • Industry-wide review of barriers to sustained careers – insecurity of employment, unpredictable contracts and working hours, expectation of unpaid overtime, issues with work/life balance, burn-out

  • Training and career retention initiatives to be part of the Scottish film studio proposals.
